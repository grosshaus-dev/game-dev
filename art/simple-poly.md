# Art Style Guide for *Simple Poly*

*Simple poly* is used to refer to 3D artwork that contains the least amount of necessary polygons needed to express the artists vision.

*Simple poly* is not *low poly*, in fact *simple poly* models can often have quite a high poly count, as all the details are modeled and not added by textures.

Rules for *simple poly* models:
- The less important or interactive an object is the larger the single flat faces should be and the less overall detail the model should contain.
- The more important or interactive and object is the smaller the flat faces should be and the more detail it should contain.
- Solid colors are to be used instead of textures; textures are not allowed.
- Normal maps may be used to add the needed detail to things like water.
- Edges may be smoothed or hard, depending on the object and the artistic vision.
