# Our Game Design Principles

*The player uses a combination of simple mechanics to interact with a complex world.*

- Non-linear creative thinking
- Deep progression
- Simulates the natural world

## Playstyles

All of the following playstyles should be applicable to any encounter. They do not have to all be equally viable; the most viable option should ideally be a combination of some or even all of them.

**Reflex based**: use your quick reflexes, and less so your brain, to overcome a situation. A *run-and-gun* approach.

**Intelligence based**: think your way through a situation, relying on smart actions rather than fast reflexes. A *the right gear for the situation* approach.

**Patience based**: use your patience to make an encounter passible, even if you don't otherwise have the reflexes or intelligence to master it. A *farm until you are over-powered* approach.


Questions to ask yourself when considering a gameplay mechanic:

1. How is the player challenged to think outside the box?
2. Does important mechanics allow room for reflex, intelligence and patience based play?
3. Does optimal gameplay require a balance of reflex, intelligence and patience based play?

## Level Design

The world doesn't need to be huge if it's dense with interaction. Stay away from trying to scale things in a realistic relation to each other. The strength of games is that we can break the bounds of reality and provide a condensed experience.

- Use dense maps with lots of interaction.
- Use a mix between areas with low interaction and ones with high interaction to keep the ones with high interaction special.
- Avoid making the player travel large distances just for the sake of it. Unless there's a point behind it, allow fast travel, even if it's 'use x amount of time and resources to travel from point a to b'.
